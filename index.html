    EdTech Game

    A project for CS4660 (Educational Technology) at Georgia Tech, developed over the span of 1-2 months. Students were tasked with forming groups and then designing a prototype of a technology that could teach a chosen topic. My group's topic was accessibility and we settled on building a prototype for a visual novel targeted at Georgia Tech students working on a long-term web development project, such as a senior capstone project. We decided on teaching web accessibility, specifically alt text.
    If you'd like to take a closer look at the project, feel free to check out our poster (first slide in the carousel above) or view the Figma prototype here.
    Here are some of my primary contributions. Note that some aspects of the game had all members contribute due to class requirements (literature review, user interviews, user testing, etc.).

  • Creating the UI and adding functionality for the customer call, visual novel dialogue, and the second coding environment
  • Interviewing potential users and conducting user testing using think-aloud protocol
  • Creating all the character illustrations and sourcing images for backgrounds online
    • After working on Current Crisis for a year, this project helped me gain a deeper understanding of educational technology while helping me develop my skills in Figma!
